How to fetch the latest SAM.gov CSV

Use the current public full Contract Opportunities export from SAM.gov, then load that file here with the picker above.

  1. Go to https://sam.gov/
  2. Go to https://sam.gov/data-services
  3. Go to https://sam.gov/data-services/Contract%20Opportunities?privacy=Public
  4. Go to https://sam.gov/data-services/Contract%20Opportunities/datagov?privacy=Public
  5. Download ContractOpportunitiesFullCSV.csv.

Each link opens in a new tab so this session stays in place. Keep the CSV outside the repo and load it directly from Downloads or another local folder.

What is NAICS?

NAICS (North American Industry Classification System) is a standardized coding system used by Federal statistical agencies to classify business establishments based on their primary economic activity.

It is the "language" used by the governments of the United States, Canada, and Mexico to collect, analyze, and publish statistical data about the North American economy.

Digits Level Example (for 333120)
1-2 Sector 33 (Manufacturing)
3 Subsector 333 (Machinery Manufacturing)
4 Industry Group 3331 (Agri., Construction, and Mining Machinery Mfg.)
5 NAICS Industry 33312 (Construction Machinery Manufacturing)
6 National Industry 333120 (U.S.-specific detail)

Note: While the official NAICS code ends at 6 digits, you may occasionally see 10-digit codes in certain government or census reports. These are known as Product Codes, which extend the 6-digit NAICS industry code to identify specific products or services within that industry.
